# Game Design Document

## Introduction

### Title: The Much-Traveled Man

### Game Summary
After crashing on an island inhabitated by cyclops, Odysseus must befriend sheep, fight cyclops, and summon gods to take rescue his kidnapped crew members

### Inspiration
I decided to create a storyline around this iconic episode of the Oddyssey, in which Odysseus defeats the cyclops on his journey home after the Trojan War because of my Classics minor. I do not plan to follow the story exactly and will exclude the word play that Odysseus uses tricks the cyclops as this doesn't suit a 2D platformer gamer. However, I will make the game more puzzle style with combat elements, as Odysseus is known for his wisdom above all.

### Player Experience
Slow and smooth gameplay will give the player time to figure out how to progress through the storyline and save Odysseus' crewmates. They must explore the island and cave, picking up useful items along the way.

### Target Audience
COSC 481 Students and Classics fans

## Concept

### Gameplay Overview
At first, Odysseus and his crew will crash onto a deserted island. The player will see Odysseus' crew mates being dragged into a cave while Odysseus is left behind but will not be able to follow and enter the cave until they figure out the puzzle on the first level, which includes befriending all the sheep in order to sneak into the cave with them. There, the player will be greeted with a gloomy environment and a limited camera view in order to mimic the darkness of a cave. The player will then traverse the cave, collecting objects that can be used to make an offering to Athena (the goddess who favors him) and summon her. Summoning Athena will grant Odysseus a new attack power. Additionally he will run into a few of his crewmates along the way to rescue. At the end, he will find the cyclops who kidnapped his crew mates and battle him. The cyclops will not be able to be defeated without the ability gained by summoning the goddess. The game will end when he rescues the rest of his crew mates. There will be minimal instructions and the player will be left to figure out what will solve the possible, although knowledge of the original story will help. Some frustration from the player can be expected.

### Theme interpretation
The driving action of the game will be for the player to rescue Odysseus' crewmates after they are kidnapped. Along the way, there will be other problems to solve, like how to enter the cave and how to defeat the final cyclops. These answers to these questions will not be given to the player but will have to figured out, although the game mechanics should give a clue to the solution. 

For the first level on the island, the camera will be encapsulating a large part of the level but when they enter the cave, it will stuck to a very small part of the screen in order to mirror what a person would be able to see if they were in a cave vs outside. For sprites, I will try to include resources that are based on Ancient Greek architecture and material culture. For example, columns might be the platforms for the player to jump on and ancient vases might be what the player has to offer to the gods. 

### Primary Mechanics 
* Attack: Use a spear to defeat cyclops
* Collect and feed: collect hay and feed to befriend the sheep
* Grab and drop: pick up other entities in the game and bring them to a new location
* Sheep tranformation: sneak closer to cyclops enemies and be able to break barrels to find hidden objects

### Secondary Mechanics
* Summon Athena: be given a powerful attack necessary to defeat the final boss cyclops
* Minimap: shows the entirety of the cave, slowly revealing different parts as the player explores
